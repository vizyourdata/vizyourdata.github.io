## Main post (LinkedIn / Mastodon / Bluesky-thread opener)

I'm entering the Pacific Dataviz Challenge 2026.

This year's theme is climate change, and I'm building an interactive story about
something more immediate than sea-level rise: what a warming ocean is doing to
what Pacific people actually eat and drink — the harvest, the rain, and the
drinking glass.

First insight from the data. Scientists have been taking the ocean's temperature
around the Pacific islands since 1850. SPC's Pacific Data Hub holds the whole
record — 176 years of it. Here it is, one stripe per year.

The ten hottest years in the entire record are the last ten. 2016 through 2025.
Every single one.

And it isn't one unlucky patch of ocean: in 20 of 21 Pacific territories, the
hottest sea ever measured has come since 2015.

More as the story comes together. Submissions close 31 August.

Data: SPC Pacific Data Hub (PDH.stat), EEZ-averaged sea-surface temperature
anomalies vs the 1993–2012 baseline.
