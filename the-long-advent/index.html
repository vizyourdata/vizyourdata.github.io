The Long Advent

It began as a single Christmas calendar in an Etsy shop — twenty-five little doors, each hiding an image of Christ. Nine Novembers later, it had become a year-round catalog. This is how it grew.

One Door

In 2017 there was one product and one shop. A Christ-centered Advent calendar — four designs, sold only on Etsy, only in November.

2017 · Etsy only · Kraft, Red & Gold, Red & Green, Black & White

A Second Spring

Then the workshop opened more doors. Easter calendars arrived in 2018; premium Simon Dewey art editions and the first framed pieces followed. The bill of designs began to multiply.

2018–2019 · + Easter · + fine-art editions · + frames

More Doors

In 2020 a brand-new line of Come Unto Christ scripture cards appeared, and the Christmas palette nearly doubled — Navy, Rustic Truck, Modern Red & Green, Poinsettia, Farmhouse.

2020–2021 · + scripture cards · the palette doubles

The Big Bloom

2022 changed everything. Prints of the artwork, stickers, and digital downloads all launched at once — and the catalog suddenly ran three times as broad as it had been in 2019.

2022 · + prints · + stickers · + digital · 13 → 42 offerings

Every Day, Not Just December

By 2024 the brand had everyday goods: a Nativity book and scripture sticker sheets that sold by the hundred — items for a Tuesday in March, not just the 1st of December.

2024 · + the book · + scripture stickers · year-round

Images of Christ

In 2025 the through-line came full circle: a fine-art line of twelve paintings of Christ — The Watcher, Radiant Peace, Beacon of Hope. The pictures that had always lived behind the little doors, now sold as art in their own right.

2025 · 12-piece fine-art line · the widest catalog yet

The through-line

It was never about the
calendar.

Strip away the twine, the cards, the magnets, the frames, and the same thing sits behind every product, every year: an image of Christ. The Advent calendar was simply the first vessel. One bestseller anchored the whole run: Traditional Red & Green, the twine kit, the workhorse in 2017 and still the workhorse in 2025.

flagship · Red & Green Twine Kit every season, 2017 → 2025 25 doors · 25 images

The shape of the year

One month carries the
other eleven.

This is a business that lives in November. The calendar is a countdown to Christmas, so the orders are too: a single towering month, a shoulder in October, an echo in December, and a faint spring rise for Easter. Christmas is what funds the experiments in everything else.

From one shop to the shelf

The handmade table found
its way onto the aisle.

For three years it was just Etsy. Then a church bazaar table, then a website, and then, in 2022, the shelf: grocery aisles at seven Macey's stores, the BYU Bookstore, Amazon, Jane.com. Wholesale meant half the price but ten times the volume. A single ward fundraiser moved 110 twine kits in one go; Kris Kringl took 96 units in one order.
