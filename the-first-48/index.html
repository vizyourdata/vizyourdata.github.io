First · The Field

The World Cup
kept getting bigger

When 13 teams turned up in Uruguay in 1930, the World Cup was an invitation. Ninety-six years later it is the planet's biggest sporting event — and it got there in just three deliberate jumps.

Teams at every men's World Cup · 1930–2026

Twenty-three tournaments. Each bar is one World Cup, from Uruguay 1930 to North America 2026. For its first half-century, the field barely moved — sixteen teams, give or take.

SOURCE · FIFA TOURNAMENT RECORDS
BARS SHOW TEAMS THAT ACTUALLY TOOK PART

Then, three jumps. 1982 went 16 → 24. 1998 went 24 → 32. Each time the door opened wider — and never closed again.

2026 adds sixteen teams at once. Half as many again. In absolute terms it is the largest single expansion in the tournament's history — a whole 1930 World Cup's worth of teams, added in one go.

Second · The New Seats

Sixteen extra places —
but not shared evenly

Adding sixteen teams meant re-drawing the map of who gets in. Every continent gained, but the new seats were aimed squarely at the regions that had long been crowded out.

Qualified nations by confederation · 2022 vs 2026

The old field, 2022. Thirty-two places, and Europe took thirteen of them. The hollow rings are where each confederation stood last time out.

CONMEBOL S. AMERICA · CONCACAF N. & C. AMERICA · OFC OCEANIA

Africa doubled — five places to ten. Asia jumped from six to nine. The continents with the most footballing nations and the fewest historic places gained the most ground.

CAF 5 → 10 · AFC 6 → 9 · UEFA 13 → 16

And Oceania, at last, has a guaranteed seat. For the first time ever, the winner of the Pacific region goes straight to the World Cup instead of into a cruel intercontinental play-off. New Zealand takes it.

Third · The Debutants

Four nations
that had never been

A bigger field means new flags. Four countries will walk out at a World Cup for the very first time — and one of them rewrites the record book on the way.

First-time qualifiers · 2026 · by national population

Meet the first-timers. Cape Verde and Curaçao, two island nations. Jordan, after decades of near-misses. And Uzbekistan — the first Central Asian country ever to make it.

Curaçao is the smallest nation ever to qualify. About 156,000 people — fewer than a mid-sized town — eclipsing Iceland's record from 2018. A Caribbean island the size of a suburb is going to the World Cup.

From a town to a nation of 36 million. Curaçao to Uzbekistan is a 230-fold spread in population — proof that the wider field reaches places the old one never could.

Fourth · The Draw

Two hundred nations,
down to forty-eight

Two years of qualifiers across six continents — more than 200 nations entering — funnel down to these 48. On 5 December 2025 a draw in Washington sorted them into twelve groups of four. Here is the whole field, and who meets whom first.

Then the draw threads them together. Each line joins the teams who meet in the group stage — a nation's first three matches. By the draw's own rules every group mixes the continents; only Europe is ever allowed to appear twice.

Every debutant drew a giant. Curaçao landed with Germany, Cabo Verde with Spain, Jordan with the holders Argentina, Uzbekistan with Portugal. The newest names, the oldest royalty — first game out.

Fifth · Three Hosts

One tournament,
three countries

No World Cup has ever been shared like this. 2026 is the first hosted by three nations at once — the United States, Mexico and Canada — across sixteen cities and four time zones. Mexico becomes the first country to host three times; Canada hosts for the very first time.

The sixteen host cities · United States · Mexico · Canada

Sixteen cities. From Vancouver in the north-west to Miami in the south-east, the tournament is plotted across a whole continent.

VENUES PLACED BY METRO LATITUDE & LONGITUDE

Three flags, one event. Eleven cities in the United States, three in Mexico, two in Canada. The first time a World Cup has been split three ways.

USA 11 · MEXICO 3 · CANADA 2 · FIRST EVER THREE-NATION HOST

It is a continental sprawl. Vancouver to Mexico City is roughly 3,800 km; the venues span tropical Miami to the mile-high altitude of Mexico City. Teams won't tour a country — they'll tour a continent.

Why this story

Every World Cup is told through its winners. But 2026 is a story you can read before a ball is kicked — in the shape of the thing: a field that doubled in a single century, redrawn to let Africa, Asia and the Pacific in, and stretched across three nations for the first time.

The numbers behind a tournament rarely make the back page. Put them on one timeline and the headline writes itself: this is the biggest, broadest, most widely shared World Cup there has ever been.

Method & honesty

  • Field bars show teams that actually took part (1938 played 15, 1950 played 13). 2026 confederation totals include the two intercontinental play-off winners, so Africa reads 10 and Asia 9 against direct allocations of 9 and 8.
  • The chord links show only group-stage pairings (each team's first three matches), not the knockout bracket. "More than 200 nations" counts FIFA members that entered the 2026 qualifying campaign.
  • Populations are orders of magnitude, not census figures. Built in the lead-up to the tournament, which kicks off June 2026.
